The best technical questions focus on functionality, performance, and security, while the best non‑technical questions focus on clarity, ease of use, and overall experience. Below is a structured set you can use for a website questionnaire.

---

🧩 Technical Questionnaire (for developers, IT teams, or technically experienced testers)

These questions help evaluate performance, architecture, security, and technical reliability. They are grounded in common usability and performance themes found in website survey research. 

Performance & Reliability
- How quickly did the website load on your device?
- Did you encounter any broken links, missing assets, or error messages?
- Were all interactive elements (forms, buttons, menus) responsive?
- Did the website behave consistently across different browsers?

Navigation & Architecture
- Was the site structure logical and easy to follow?
- Did the URL structure make sense and reflect the content hierarchy?
- Were redirects, canonical tags, and metadata implemented correctly?

Security & Compliance
- Did you notice any security warnings (mixed content, certificate issues)?
- Does the website follow secure practices (HTTPS, secure forms)?
- Are cookies and privacy notices clear and compliant with regulations?

Mobile & Cross‑Device Compatibility
- Did the website render correctly on mobile/tablet?
- Were touch targets (buttons, menus) appropriately sized? 
- Did any layout elements break on smaller screens?

Content & Data Integrity
- Was dynamic content (APIs, databases) loading correctly?
- Did search, filters, or sorting features work as expected?
- Were images and videos optimized and loading without delay?

---

🌿 Non‑Technical Questionnaire (for general users)

These questions focus on usability, design, clarity, and overall satisfaction, reflecting common patterns in usability surveys. 

General Experience
- What was your first impression when you entered the website? 
- What did you like most about the website?
- What frustrated you the most?

Ease of Use
- Were you able to find what you were looking for without help? 
- How easy was it to navigate the website?
- Did you ever feel “lost” while browsing?

Design & Visual Appeal
- Do you find the colors, layout, and typography appealing?
- Is the content easy to read and understand at a glance? 
- Does the design feel modern and trustworthy?

Content Quality
- Was the information clear, accurate, and helpful?
- Did you find all the information you expected?
- Was anything confusing or unclear?

Mobile Experience
- Was the mobile layout easy to use?
- Were buttons and menus easy to tap? 

Satisfaction & Suggestions
- How likely are you to return to this website?
- What would you improve if you could change one thing?
- Do you have any additional feedback?

---

📌 Quick Comparison Table

| Category | Technical Questions | Non‑Technical Questions |
|---------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| Focus | Performance, security, architecture | Usability, clarity, satisfaction |
| Audience | Developers, QA, IT | General users, customers |
| Goal | Identify system issues | Improve user experience |
| Examples | Load speed, errors, mobile rendering | Ease of navigation, design appeal |
