Task 3a – What you should be doing
Before you build anything:
Revisit Task 1.
Pull out and record client needs, project aims, user stories and KPIs.
Decide how you will measure whether your web app meets those needs and KPIs.
Remember the purpose of this is to give you something to talk about in task 3B!
Then split the feedback work into three clear tasks:
Non‑technical user questionnaire (20 questions - 10 people to survey)
Technical user questionnaire (20 questions - 10 people to survey)
User observations (Plan the user journey through the app - get at least 3 full observations and record them)
Next:
Plan each of the three tasks properly (what you are measuring, why, and how).
You must not use AI at any point.
You already have lots of usable information in your GitHub – use it.
Build your questionnaires using Google Forms or Microsoft Forms.
Record the use of your app. 
Collect the responses - use each other as technical users.
We will provide other students for the observation activity. We will aim to do this on Friday. They can also act as the non-technical users if necessary.
Evidence is critical:
Screenshot everything.
Save all responses, observation notes, and exports.
Put all data and screenshots in your folder.
You will not have internet access in Task 3b, so if it isn’t saved, it doesn’t exist.
Stay within supervised conditions and focus on gathering useful, measurable feedback that can inform future development.
